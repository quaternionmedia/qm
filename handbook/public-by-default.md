# Handbook — Public by Default

Work ships in the open unless a specific, named reason requires otherwise.
The burden of proof sits on *closing*, not opening.

**Recognized reasons to close:** client confidentiality (contractually
scoped), credentials and operational secrets, embargoed security fixes.
"Not polished yet" is not a reason; the public repositories' history is the
precedent that it never was.

**What this policy carries:** the public-fork carrying mechanism of the
open-license record and the readability of decision records to clients and
community both stand on this default.

**Promotion path.** This is business policy, not a decision record — its
exceptions are contractual and it has no architectural alternatives to weigh.
If its boundary ever needs adjudicable teeth (a dispute over whether
something qualifies for closing, a pattern of exception creep), it is
promoted to an org record through the normal drafting process, and this page
becomes a pointer.
